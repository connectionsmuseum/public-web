Visiting the museum

Hours

The museum is open to the public every Sunday from 10 am to 3 pm, unless otherwise noted on this page.

The suggested donation is $10 for adults and $5 youths aged 12 to 18.

While we are closed to the public for our volunteer work day, we're typically around to take donations on Tuesdays from about 11am to 3pm, but please email us in advance.

Tours

Docent guided tours start on demand, usually with little or no wait. Please note that our doors close at 2:30pm to permit visitors enough time to conclude their tour. A quick tour can take 1 hour, but typical tours take up to two hours. For those with a keen technical interest, 3 hours in the museum is common.

Returning visitors are welcome to explore the public areas of the museum on their own pace.

Directions

We are located in the Georgetown neighborhood of Seattle, Washington, at 7000 E Marginal Way S, Seattle, WA 98108. It is an active central office in a large concrete building. Our entrance is around the side, a blue door under a yellow awning.

Sadly, bike parking is limited at this time, but we are located conveniently on King County Metro Route 60 and 124, just a short walk from Stop ID #40871 and #46025.

Our parking lot entrance is on Corson Avenue. Space is often limited, but can usually be found nearby at South Seattle Community College.

Other Information

Children

We ask that parents with children under twelve visit the museum first, before bringing their children, to determine the suitability of museum exhibits for younger visitors.

Pets

We only allow trained service animals inside the museum.

COVID

Masks are welcomed, but not required.

Safety

Live electricity is exposed in many non-obvious places, and rotating machinery can catch on clothing. Such is the nature of running equipment as old as ours-some is over 100 years old! We do our best to ensure a safe environment despite this, and all tours should begin with a quick safety briefing.

Many of our artifacts are old and irreplaceable, but with just a few exceptions, we prefer to not put them away "under glass." So our simple rule is this: Please ask one of our volunteer guides before you pick up items on display or interact with our machines.
